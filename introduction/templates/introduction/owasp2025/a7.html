A7: Authentication Failures

What is this vulnerability?

Authentication failures cover weaknesses in how applications verify identity: weak passwords, missing multi-factor authentication, flawed session management, credential stuffing, insecure “remember me” tokens, predictable session identifiers, or broken password reset flows.

Real World Example

Credential-stuffing campaigns use billions of leaked username-password pairs from other breaches. Services without MFA, rate limits, or breached-password checks see mass account takeover even when their own code has no injection bugs.

Impact

  • Account takeover and impersonation of users or administrators
  • Fraud, data theft, and access to connected systems
  • High support cost and reputational damage from recovery efforts

Mitigation

  • Enable MFA where possible and enforce strong password policies with breach checks
  • Use secure cookies (HttpOnly, Secure, SameSite) and rotate session IDs on privilege change
  • Rate-limit login and recovery endpoints; monitor for stuffing and spraying
