A3: Software Supply Chain Failures

What is this vulnerability?

Software supply chain failures occur when attackers compromise third-party code, package registries, build systems, CI/CD pipelines, or signing processes. A single malicious dependency, typosquatted package, or hijacked release pipeline can propagate to every downstream project that trusts that artifact.

Real World Example

The SolarWinds Orion incident showed how attackers could insert malicious code into a vendor’s trusted build and distribute signed updates to thousands of customers, demonstrating systemic risk when upstream integrity fails.

Real World Example from OWASP Python-Honeypot

The codecov dependency (version 2.1.12) was removed from PyPI and archived by maintainers, introducing supply-chain risk into the OWASP Python-Honeypot project. This is a real example of A03:2025 Software Supply Chain failure.

Impact

  • Widespread distribution of backdoors or malware through trusted channels
  • Long dwell time because artifacts may appear legitimate and signed
  • Difficult remediation across many teams and deployment targets

Mitigation

  • Pin dependencies, verify checksums, and maintain an SBOM with SCA tooling
  • Protect CI/CD with branch protections, secret management, and signed artifacts
  • Monitor advisories and replace or fork dependencies that become unavailable or untrusted
